## â—ˆ The Vision

### The Challenge

Leibniz believed that if we could identify the *truly primitive* conceptsâ€”ideas that cannot be decomposed furtherâ€”we could:

1. **Assign each a symbol** (or prime number)
2. **Compose complex ideas** by combining primitives
3. **Resolve disputes** by calculation: *"Calculemus!"* (Let us calculate!)

### Our Approach

An AI reasoning agent operates as a **multi-role system**:

| Role | Purpose | Temperature |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **PROPOSER** | Generate candidate primitives from philosophical analysis | 0.7 (creative) |
| **CRITIC** | Attempt to decompose, find circularity, challenge primitiveness | 0.3 (rigorous) |
| **REFINER** | Integrate validated primitives, maintain consistency | 0.5 (balanced) |
| **META-REASONER** | Reflect on the process, adjust strategy | 0.5 (balanced) |
| **ARCHIVIST** | Log everythingâ€”this is the soul of the project | N/A |

## â—ˆ The Reasoning Archive

> **This is the heart of the project.**

Every thought, every decision, every dead end is logged. The reasoning archive captures the *inner monologue* of a system grappling with the foundations of human thought.

### Why This Matters

1. **Transparency**: See exactly why each primitive was accepted or rejected
2. **Meta-Research**: Study how AI systems reason about abstract concepts
3. **Content Generation**: Turn these logs into blog posts, papers, explorations
4. **Reproducibility**: Replay the reasoning at any point

```yaml
  iteration: 42

      - claim: "Causation might reduce to temporal priority + regularity"

                   But this requires understanding of possible worlds,
                   which seems MORE complex than causation itself."
        verdict: "This decomposes causation into something harder to ground."
```

- `proposer_log.md` â€” What candidates were generated and why
- `critic_log.md` â€” How candidates were challenged
- `refiner_log.md` â€” What was integrated and how

### Explore the Alphabet

The `/alphabet/primitives/detailed/` directory contains full entries for each accepted primitive, including:

- Formal and informal definitions
- Justification for primitiveness
- Relationships to other primitives
- Leibniz correspondence (if any)

### Try the Calculus

