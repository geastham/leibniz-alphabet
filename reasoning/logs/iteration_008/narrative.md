## The Candidates

### 1. INDETERMINACY (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **indeterminacy** - true randomness beyond mere possibility.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Indeterminacy = Possibility + Negation(Causal Determination)**
- An event is indeterminate when multiple outcomes are possible AND prior causes don't select among them
- True randomness = uncaused possibility
- This captures quantum indeterminacy and libertarian free will

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.75 confidence

**Key Insight**: Indeterminacy is possibility in a causal vacuum. The "random" is where causation doesn't reach among possibilities.

### 2. CONTINUITY (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **continuity** - the property of being unbroken, the continuum.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Continuity = Negation(Existence(Composition-Minima))**
- A continuum has no atoms - no smallest parts
- Continuous = infinitely divisible
- Any part can be further divided

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.70 confidence

**Key Insight**: The continuum is characterized by infinite divisibility. What makes something continuous is the absence of indivisible minima.

### 3. SIMILARITY (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **similarity** - the relation of resemblance.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Similarity = Identity(Quality(X), Quality(Y)) for some respect**
- X resembles Y when they share a quality
- Partial similarity = identity in some qualities
- Complete similarity = identity in all qualities

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.85 confidence

**Key Insight**: Similarity is shared quality. Resemblance is partial identity - being the same in some respects.
