## The Candidates

### 1. REFLEXIVITY (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **reflexivity** - the capacity for self-reference.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Reflexivity = Intentionality(Bearer-of-Phenomenality)**
- The "self" that can be referenced IS the bearer of phenomenal states
- Self-reference = directing intentionality toward the phenomenal subject
- Self-awareness = phenomenality + intentionality(self)

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.75 confidence

**Key Insight**: We don't need a separate primitive for self-reference. The "self" is grounded in phenomenality (that which has experiences), and self-reference is just intentionality directed at that subject.

### 2. SIGNIFICATION (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **signification** - the standing-for relation of symbols.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Signification = Composition(Minds) + Normativity + Intentionality + Possibility**
- Convention = shared (composition + alterity) normative (normativity) intentionality (what we mean)
- The arbitrariness of signs = possibility (could have been otherwise)
- A symbol signifies X when multiple minds normatively intend it to mean X

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.70 confidence

**Key Insight**: Language doesn't require its own primitive. Signification is grounded in shared normative intentionality. Convention is what we collectively and normatively mean by a sign.

### 3. FORCE (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **force** - the capacity to produce effects.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Force = Quality + Possibility + Causation**
- Force is the dispositional quality toward causing effects
- To have force = to have the quality such that one WOULD cause under certain conditions
- Power = dispositional causation

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.80 confidence

**Key Insight**: Force reduces to dispositional causation. The "power" to cause is the quality of being disposed to produce effects. No separate primitive needed for the material domain.
