## The Candidates

### 1. NORMATIVITY (ACCEPTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **normativity** - the dimension of ought, should, must.

The CRITIC confirmed its primitiveness:
- **Decomposition**: The is/ought gap is real. No descriptive primitive generates prescriptive content.
- **Independence**: Normativity is orthogonal to all our descriptive primitives.
- **Necessity**: Without it, we cannot express ethics, epistemology, or evaluation.

**Verdict**: ACCEPT with 0.78 confidence

**Key Insight**: Normativity is the ONLY genuinely new dimension we've added since iteration 4. Everything else derives! The is/ought gap is a real seam in the space of concepts.

### 2. TRUTH (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **truth** - correspondence between representation and reality.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Truth = Intentionality + Existence**
- A representation is TRUE iff what it's ABOUT (intentionality) EXISTS (existence)
- "Snow is white" is true iff the state of affairs the sentence represents exists

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.68 confidence

**Key Insight**: Truth is derivable! We don't need a primitive for truth - the combination of intentionality (aboutness) and existence (reality) suffices.

### 3. VALENCE (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **valence** - the positive/negative dimension of experience.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Valence = Quality + Normativity**
- Pleasure is a QUALITY that is normatively POSITIVE (ought to be pursued)
- Pain is a QUALITY that is normatively NEGATIVE (ought to be avoided)

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.65 confidence

**Key Insight**: This rejection DEPENDS on accepting normativity. Normativity's power extends beyond ethics - it captures the evaluative dimension of experience itself.

## Philosophical Reflection

Iteration 5 marks a crucial milestone: **the evaluative dimension enters the alphabet**.

Hume famously argued you cannot derive OUGHT from IS. Our framework confirms this:
- 10 descriptive primitives could not generate normativity
- Normativity had to be added as its own primitive
- But once added, it unlocks valence and evaluative concepts generally

The is/ought gap is not a logical curiosity but a **genuine seam in conceptual space**.

With normativity, we can finally express:
- "You should keep your promises"
- "This belief is correct"
- "That experience is good"

The Leibnizian alphabet approaches completion. 11 primes may suffice for the alphabet of human thought.
