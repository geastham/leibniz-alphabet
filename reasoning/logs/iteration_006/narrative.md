## The Candidates

### 1. PHENOMENALITY (ACCEPTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **phenomenality** - the qualitative, experiential character of conscious states.

The CRITIC confirmed its primitiveness through rigorous analysis:
- **Decomposition**: Phenomenality resists all major reductive programs (functionalism, representationalism, IIT). The explanatory gap persists.
- **Circularity**: Definition uses phenomenal vocabulary, but this is EXPECTED for genuine primitives - we can only point to qualia ostensively.
- **Redundancy**: Intentionality gives aboutness, not felt quality. Quality gives "what kind" but not experiential character. No combination of existing primitives captures "what it's like."
- **Cultural Variance**: Universal - all cultures recognize felt experience.
- **Parsimony**: Essential - without it, cannot express consciousness, qualia, sentience.

**Verdict**: ACCEPT with 0.9 confidence

**Key Insight**: The hard problem of consciousness is hard precisely because phenomenality is PRIMITIVE - it cannot be reduced to or derived from non-phenomenal concepts.

### 2. ALTERITY (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **alterity** - the primitive recognition of otherness, specifically another subject.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Alterity = Intentionality(Phenomenality in Negation(Self-Identity))**
- To recognize an "other" = to recognize that phenomenality exists in something not identical to self
- The concept of "other-as-subject" decomposes once we have phenomenality

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.75 confidence

**Key Insight**: The social domain may not need its own primitive! With phenomenality, we can derive alterity, which unlocks intersubjectivity.

### 3. AWARENESS (REJECTED)

The PROPOSER suggested **awareness** - direct epistemic access or acquaintance.

The CRITIC found a derivation:
- **Awareness = Phenomenality + Intentionality**
- To be aware of X = to have a phenomenal state directed at X
- "Pure awareness" (contentless) = just phenomenality alone

**Verdict**: REJECT with 0.8 confidence

**Key Insight**: Awareness is not a distinct primitive but phenomenal intentionality. The epistemic relation of "having content present" IS having phenomenal intentional states.

## The Mind Domain Expands

With phenomenality, the mind domain now has TWO primitives:
- **Intentionality (P007)**: The ABOUTNESS of mental states (what they're directed toward)
- **Phenomenality (P012)**: The FELT QUALITY of mental states (what they're like)

This distinction is crucial:
- A philosophical zombie has intentionality without phenomenality
- Pure meditation states may have phenomenality without intentionality
- Normal conscious experience has BOTH

The combination (phenomenal intentionality) = awareness.

## Philosophical Reflection

Iteration 6 tackles the hardest problem in philosophy of mind.

**Chalmers' Hard Problem**: Why does information processing give rise to subjective experience? Our framework shows this question arises because phenomenality is PRIMITIVE - it cannot be derived from functional, informational, or intentional concepts.

**The Explanatory Gap**: The gap between physical/functional description and phenomenal experience is not an epistemic limitation but a reflection of phenomenality's irreducibility.

**Derivation Power**: With 12 primitives, we can now derive:
- Consciousness = phenomenality
- Awareness = phenomenality + intentionality
- Qualia = phenomenality applied to specific qualities
- Empathy = intentionality directed at another's phenomenality
- Intersubjectivity = composition of phenomenal beings
